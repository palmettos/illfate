THIS WAY TO MAYBE

The game industry has become way too obsessed with control: keeping players retained, monetized, measured, and safely moving through systems that are designed to be predictable. But the magic of games was never supposed to be that. The magic is when a game gives players a living world—a set of systems, tools, objects, and communities—and says, “Go discover what this can become.” Too many games today turn economies into shops, choices into progression tracks, identity into cosmetics, and community into something managed from above instead of built from within. Even indie games can fall into this trap when they copy the same loops, rewards, templates, and algorithm-friendly aesthetics instead of using freedom to try something genuinely new. I think the future belongs to games that can bring people together, then give them worlds deep enough to create real stories, real economies, real play styles, and communities with memory. And now, with AI lowering the distance between imagination and execution, the old permission structures matter less than ever. This is the moment to stop waiting, stop asking for approval, and start building games with enough beauty, conviction, and courage to make the medium feel alive again.
